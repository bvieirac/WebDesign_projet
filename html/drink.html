Drinks

Madeira is an island that offers a range of drinks that have become a part of Madeira's tradition. Drinks such as “Poncha”, “Nikita”, “Coral” and the world famous “Madeira Wine”. These alcoholic drinks are traditionally served at bars, restaurants and at local and festive events. Here are some suggestions for traditional drinks you must try in Madeira during your stay on the island!

Poncha

The “Poncha” is a traditional alcoholic beverage from Madeira, made with sugar cane rum, bee honey and lemon juice. It can also be prepared with other fruit juices, like orange or mandarin, but the lemon juice is in the original version. It is mixed with a tool officially created for this purpose, called a muskot “Caralhinho”.

Nikita

The “Nikita” is a typical drink of Câmara de Lobos, but it's popular all over the island. Nikita is made of ice cream (various flavors), sugar, pineapple rings, and an alcoholic beverage, such as white wine or white beer, all of which are well mixed until creamy.

Coral beer

Madeira has a great local beer called “Coral“. This is the beer you order in Madeira! It is made from the best malts of Czech origin, fermented and cured at low temperatures. It has a pale gold color, a clean, aromatic flavor, a body of light and a pleasant aroma, highly esteemed by locals and visitors alike.

Madeira's Wine

Over the centuries, Madeira's Wine won worldwide reputation, and both the island and the wine have become synonymous with quality. It is a unique wine, intense, sweet and fresh, which gets better over the years. Madeira wine is characterized by its taste of caramelized sugar, resulting from the aging in wooden casks.

Brisa

The “Brisa” is the name of a carbonated drink, a very popular brand in Madeira and it has a variety of flavors: passion fruit, apple, lemon and orange.

Cider

The production of cider(sidra) is the result of the alcoholic fermentation of apple juice. It can be served in 2 ways, cold and hot. The cold comes only from the fermentation of apple juice and the hot one has its flavor and scent modified by the heat.

Ginja

The “Ginja” is a traditional liqueur from Curral das Freiras (Nun's valley) made through the infusion of Ginjas, in alcohol and adding caramel along with other ingredients.This liqueur is one of the favorites of many inhabitants of the island.

Sugar Cane Rum

With a history dating back half a century, here you can find some of the oldest sugar cane mills. Currently, almost all sugarcane production is destined for the manufacture of sugarcane honey and agricultural rum , traditionally “Cane Rum”.With distinctive and unique aromas, is recognized by several specialized tasters as a product of unquestionable quality.
