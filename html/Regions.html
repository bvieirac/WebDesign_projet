Regions

Although it is small, Madeira is rich in majestic scenery and rare beauty. Go and explore the charms and delights of this floating garden! The main places to visit in the Madeira Islands are Funchal, the capital of the archipelago, the east coast (Santa Cruz and Machico), the west coast (Câmara de Lobos, Ribeira Brava, Ponta do Sol and Calheta), the north coast (Porto Moniz; São Vicente and Santana) and Porto Santo. Delight yourself with the countless views, on an island of such outstanding natural beauty!

Funchal

Funchal is the capital of the Madeira archipelago which dates back about 600 years. Funchal is the largest tourist, commercial and cultural center in the entire Madeira archipelago. The city offers a wide range of leisure activities, from visits to its churches and other monuments, walks in its fantastic gardens, in Funchal Marina with the colorful boats, shopping and night outs to bars and clubs.

Machico

Located 22km from funcal, this municipality has great historical significance. It was here that Gonçalves Zarco and Tristão Vaz Teixeira landed in 1419, when they discovered Madeira.

Santana

Santana is located on the north coast of Madeira island. Because the municipality was inaccessible by sea and land, its inhabitants have preserved features that have not suffered outside influences. One example that comes from this isolation is the typical architectural style of their houses with thatched roofs known as “casinhas de Santana” (little houses of Santana).

Ponta do Sol

Situated on the south coast of the island, Ponta do Sol is considered the hottest municipality of the island and it is also here where the sun shines for more hours. This municipality has the Paul da Serra, which is distinguished for being the only plateau on the island of Madeira, and this at an altitude of 1,500 m. This site has unique natural features, as nuclei of indigenous vegetation, wildlife species and migratory birds.

Porto Moniz

Located in the north of the island of Madeira,Porto Moniz is connected to the rest of the island , with the emblematic road connecting Porto Moniz to São Vicente. This road is occasionally crossed by waterfalls that flow into the This is considered one of the most beautiful roads in wood. For safety reasons, today there are tunnels that shorten the distance. You can also visit the gorgeous pools in Porto Moniz.

Porto Santo

Porto Santo is the second largest island of the Autonomous Region of Madeira, The island is known for its large fine yellow sandy beach, stretching close to 9 km, running along the island's entire southern coastline. Above all, it offers peace and quiet and a catching charm, which doesn't leave any visitor indifferent. A particular highlight is the islands' sea water temperature,it is really warm all year round..

Camâra de Lobos

Câmara de Lobos is on the south coast of the Madeira Island. The name of this city comes from the fact that at the time of the discovery of the island loads of sea lions were sighted at that cove that still retains the same configuration.This land of fishermen, whose main expertise is the fishing for black scabbardfish, owes its fame to the fishermen who paint the bay with their picturesque and very distinctive boats - the "Xavelhas".
